Ontology 2.0

This Information Model defines a high-level and protocol-agnostic ontology of the DestinE Platform.

The DestinE Platform Information Model offers and guarantees:

  • compatibility and interoperability, as its structured, semantic annotation ensures Registered Service Providers to use the ontology as a reference at system-level or as a driver for Data Model aspects
  • to enforce a common terminology (vocabulary) across the DestinE Platform landscape and in the relevant documentation
  • free and open access to the definition of all the classes and properties of the DestinE Platform entities, its participants, infrastructure components and processes, on a documental level
  • an overview of DestinE Platform design correspondence and alignement to SIMPL architectural layers

DestinE Platform Information Model

In the following Sections, we detail our proposed information model applicable to the DestinE Platform.

This Information Model describes the fundamental concepts of the DestinE Platform, which constitutes an Ecosystem of services. The DestinE Platform solution enables and ensures interoperability between its participants to share resources in a well-specified manner. To maximise interoperability with external entities and among the services proposed on the platform, the definition of an information model will support potential new Service Providers: in fact, the information model describes the key concepts to achieve this goal, remaining agnostic to the specific implementation details of each participant.

Facets

Following the approach of International Data Spaces Association, the overall view can be divided into logical groups of concepts (hereinafter called facets), addressing different static and dynamic aspects of the DestinE Platform. Most of the facets are named after the key concept they represent.

Participant

The Participant facet comprehends the data, application, service and infrastructure providers and consumers of DestinE Platform, allowed to pool together resources to create more business value, efficient resource usage, reducing costs and the duplication of efforts made towards a common goal.

A Participant is a legal or natural person assuming a role (or more than one role) in DestinE Platform framework. A Participant can be an organization (or organizational unit) or an individual. If the Participant is an individual (Person), he or she may assume a specific role in the corresponding organization. The Participant concept may indicate the type of business and the domain in which operates.

Each Participant can be assigned to one or more unambiguously defined sites. Site information comprises the name and address of the site as well as geo-location information. It is particularly important in cases in which specific rules (e.g., national law) apply, affecting, for example, the data usage control policy. Pending the publication of the architectural principles of the European SIMPL Initiative, four types of actors, briefly summarized, are considered as follows:

  • Data providers hold valuable data that they share to the Ecosystem
  • Infrastructure providers allow users to run applications on their infrastructure
  • Application providers share their applications and algorithms towards interested end users
  • End users consume resources


This summary heavily simplifies the interactions and relations between Participants. Complex processes, such as usage agreements, accompany these interactions. These roles and their relevant playing field are translated in the Information Model as object properties between the Participant Class and involved ones, such as consumer or provider of a Resource or Service that is the subject matter of the Contract (see Contract); sovereign, publisher, creator (of a Resource); maintainer or curator (of an Infrastructure Component). Moreover, the user type according to DestinE Platform user taxonomy is specified for each Person thanks to the DestinE Platform User Type data property.



Resource

The Resource facet encompasses the building blocks to enable the exchange of data and applications. The Platform offers data consumers the means to access different types of data from different providers, enabling interoperability between providers and consumers. The facet contains the services to share as well as managed data and applications, and perform basic analysis. It encompasses the SIMPL Data Layer. A Resource Class is defined and identified as an abstract concept covering what can be provided and/or consumed in the framework of the DestinE Platform. Principal resources are Data Resource, Applications, Services, and Cloud Resources as well this latter belonging to the Infrastructure facet.

A Data Resource is an identifiable, valuable, digital (non-physical) commodity exchanged among Participants by means of DestinE Platform Services. Data Reosources can be:

  • Variable, the atomic item constituting a Dataset, Data Collection, belonging to a Program Subset, forming a Program.
  • User Generated Data, i.e. data generated by DestinE Platform Participants (type: Registered). These data will not belong to the DestinE Platform Data Portfolio initially, but can be added to it only after the registration of the Service that provide them into the Service Registry. Indeed, the documentation of all DestinE Platform Registered Services will be described and maintained updated in the DestinE Platform Service Portfolio, in a dedicated public document available to end users.


A DestinE Platform Service may have a Data Portfolio that describes its data offer.

An Application is a software package, library or algorithm that performs a specific function, that can be installed within the infrastructure to communicate or process data on behalf of the Participants.

We define a DestinE Platform Service as a specific function operated by a Service Provider based on a service-level agreement. Each of these services is presented with a list of gallery items and video tutorials, avaiable in the ID&OS. In the context of the DestinE Platform, Services can be of different type:
  • Core Services, implemented and operated under this contract.
  • Federated Services, Services with an existing active user community that becomes accessible for DestinE Platform users via their existing identity, after completing the Federation Procedure.
  • Onboarded Services, become part of the DestinE Platform ecosystem after successfully completing the Onboarding process


DestinE Platform Services can expose to users one or more Service Interfaces, depending on the scope. In the case of Onboarded Service onto the Runtime Platform, they should be specified during the onboarding process, verified and accepted by DestinE Platform Consortium according to security conditions, and then clearly indicated to users in the Service Registrys description of the new Service.

Integration criteria with Platform Management Services, DestinE Platform Infrastructure and Runtime Platform are stated in the DestinE Platform Operations Concept Document, for each type of Service.



Infrastructure

The Infrastructure facet allows DestinE Platform users to discover, utilize, and manage infrastructure services offered by infrastructure providers. It corresponds to the SIMPL Infrastructure Layer and depicts the dependencies and interactions between the different infrastructure building blocks on a high level.

The Classes belonging to this facet enable DestinE Platform users to easily find and provision the necessary computing and storage resources to execute their workloads in a secure and energy-efficient way.

This facet allows the DestinE Platform to connect to third-party infrastructure services, such that Participants can execute Services on them. DestinE Platform offers a Runtime Platform (PaaS) to hosts its Core services and optionally new onboarded or federated services relying on OVH Cloud Resources (IaaS and CaaS). They comprehend: Computing Instances, Storage, Network Database, Container services, Data and AI.

The Class for HPC refers to DestinE Platform capacity to address Multi-cloud and HPC infrastructure providers, for example adding new external resources from HPC operators - designed to support the execution of compute-intensive tasks.

As in the first phase of DestinE Platform no other cloud provider nor HPC is foreseen, these capacities will not be used in the current stage, however it is important to highlight how the platform will be able to handle these requirements at later stages.

Security capabilities are constantly active. The agreed level of security encryption and integrity on every data transmission or service deployment, granted for the consumer at any time, are traced thanks to specific data properties of the infrastructure components.



Interaction

The Interaction facet vertically spans the Resource and Infrastructure ones. Covering the SIMPL Administration and Governance layers, it provides services that are required for the well-functioning of all the other facets, as well as the DestinE Platform as a whole. These services are the Platform Management Services, explicitly reported as sub-Classes of DestinE Platform Service class which implements pillars functions such as security, identification and access control, monitoring, and more.

Among them, the Identification & Authorization Management (IAM) Service manages and validates the digital identity of DestinE Platform Participants. Related to the security capabilities, the functions gathered by IAM Service will be constantly required whenever an end user or a provider accesses DestinE Platform Services. In this sense, every relation of the user with service or infrastructure provisioning is closely screened by the IAM Service ensuring identification, authentication and authorization. Client and relevant Client Role, configurable via IAM Service, are identified while identity management is linked to Person class.

The Service Registry catalogues all applications, services, data and software available for re-use by Third-Party users. The DestinE Platform Core Services, The Service Registry catalogues all applications, services, data and software available for re-use by Third-Party users. The DestinE Platform Core Services, Onboarded Services and Federated Services are reported. Moreover, it establishes a public procedure to manage DestinE Platform Services onboarding. Following the procedure, potential Service Providers, eager to register their own applications and services, will be supported in the onboarding activity and resource integration.

The Information Dissemination & Onboarding Support service takes care of the DestinE Platform Web Portal, the entry point for users approaching to DestinE Platform, as well as of the related user experience and community engagement activities. The website is where users log in to have access to DestinE Platform Services, and where a Support Page allows them to raise User Support request by both a logged-in user and a not-logged one. The help request will be redirected to the Ticketing System, on the Jira platform, and will be taken over by the Service Desk. The Service Desk is available for users to interact with DestinE Platform and Community by submitting issues, support and/or information request, and has the responsibility to manage the relevant ticketing system. Moreover, it oversees user feedbacks analysis and reporting relevant to user support handling and services (see Feedback Class).

The Service Desk will also take care of the interaction with the DestinE elements and/or third-party application services as part of the anomaly and maintenance management processes. The User Support Team will raise internal tickets for specific Issues, e.g., when they discover anomalies or faults in the system or want to share suggestions on improvements for workflows or processes.

So, not only user support requests are tracked in the Ticketing System, but all the issues that can be raised by DestinE Platform stakeholders (users and internal teams). Specific projects (see Ticketing System Project) have been defined in JIRA to manage each Issue Type (see Issue data property): Anomaly; User Request; Operational Anomaly; Story; SPR; Maintenance Request; Security Incident; Change Request.

The Clearing House records the transaction received by each Service, relevant to active users. Information relevant to a Transaction, the Service that allows it and the Participant who performs it are stored as transaction data into the Clearing House

Transaction Class collect information relevant to transactions, i.e. a completed event, that can be allowed by a Service. Indeed, completed event implies a correct operation provided to a Person, specifically an Active Users, i.e., individual or service accounts who have registered with DestinE Platform and utilize DestinE Platform services to access resources within a time interval. Active user concept is traced in the relevant object property.

Events, i.e., any occurrence that is relevant to a service, are stored and aggregated by the Monitoring Service to monitor DestinE Platform Services, Cloud Resources and Runtime Platform performances, aggregated into Statistics, to provide up-to-date Reports of the achieved operations performances. Monitoring Service is also in charge of the computation of KPIs.



Regulation

The Regulation facet includes also concepts underlying business interactions among the DestinE Platform Participants, i.e. the interchange and consumption of Resources according to defined Regulations.

Main Classes included in this facet are Rules, Actions and Contracts.

The Rules specify Actions that an involved Participant is obliged, permitted or prohibited to perform with respect to Resource. An Action is associated with the Resource via the action property in a Rule.

The Rule provides the specific interpretations of the Action. For example: an Action is permitted to be exercised on the target Resource when related to a Permission. When related to a Prohibition, the Action indicates the operation that is prohibited to be exercised on the target Resource. When related to a Duty, the Action indicates the agreed operation that is obligatory to be fulfilled by a Participant.

A Contract express Permissions, Prohibitions and Duties related to the usage of Resource. It defines a validity Period and formal Rules agreed upon by Participants involved in the provision, or subsequent usage of the Resource. Contracts formalize the expectations on behaviour of involved Participants in a declarative, technology-agnostic way. The perpetual control and enforcement of such specification level policies may involve the inception of governance processes or, when appropriate the deployment of a technological solution.
